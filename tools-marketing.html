Marketing and sales tools compared

Canva

Magic Studio (Magic Write, Magic Design)

What it does

Magic Write drafts ad copy, social captions, and marketing text inside Canva's design editor; Magic Design auto-generates social posts, presentations, and video from a prompt or an uploaded asset.

Best use case

Small marketing teams or solo creators who need quick, on-brand visual-plus-copy pairs — a social post or simple ad creative — without separate copywriting and design tools.

Limitations

Free-tier AI usage is metered monthly, not unlimited, and higher-quality "premium" AI models consume that allowance faster than standard ones.

Free tier

Canva's free plan includes 200 uses of standard AI tools or 20 uses of premium AI tools per month; Magic Write is documented separately as offering a free query allowance (Canva's own help page states 25 — treat as approximate, since Canva has changed this before).

Privacy

On Free and Pro plans, AI training on your content is on by default but can be opted out in Privacy Settings; Teams/Business/Enterprise/Education accounts are never used for AI training.

HubSpot

Breeze (AI content assistant)

What it does

Breeze Assistant drafts marketing emails, blog content, and sales follow-ups grounded in your actual CRM data, not generic context. Separate paid "Agents" automate prospecting and customer outreach.

Best use case

Businesses already using HubSpot's free CRM who want AI drafting tied to real contact and deal records, rather than a disconnected chatbot.

Limitations

The free tier gets Assistant plus basic embedded features only — agentic automation (prospecting, customer, data agents) requires paid plans and per-action credit spend ($1/lead, $0.50/resolution, $0.10/answer).

Free tier (confirmed)

Breeze Assistant and 100+ embedded AI features are included in HubSpot's free CRM edition at no cost.

Privacy

States third-party AI providers are contractually prohibited from training on your data, backed by SOC 2 Type 2 audits, with published model cards on its own Trust site.

Google

Gemini (free, for ad copy & marketing drafting)

What it does

The free Gemini chatbot can draft ad copy, email campaigns, and social posts on request; inside Google Ads itself, Gemini-powered asset and copy suggestions are built into standard campaign tools at no added tool cost.

Best use case

Solo marketers or small businesses wanting a genuinely free, general-purpose AI writer for ad variations, without a dedicated marketing-AI subscription.

Limitations

Less purpose-built for marketing than Canva or HubSpot — no CRM grounding, no brand-kit memory, just general drafting ability.

Free tier

Gemini's core chatbot is free with any Google account, no credit card. Ad-copy generation inside Google Ads carries no premium fee beyond normal ad spend — verify this directly in Google Ads' own help center before relying on it, since this claim traces to secondary coverage rather than an Ads-specific primary source.

Privacy

Follows Gemini's general privacy notice — see the Writing tools page for the full detail.

Jasper — flagged, not recommended, for this free-tools list

Jasper is one of the best-known names in AI marketing copy, so it's worth naming here for completeness — but it has no meaningful free tier. It offers only a 7-day free trial on its Pro plan, requiring a credit card up front, with paid plans starting at $59/month billed annually. Given this site's free-only standard, Jasper doesn't belong on the recommendation list above, but if you're evaluating paid options, it's a legitimate one to compare against. Official pricing ↗

Flagged during research, not smoothed over: Canva's exact Magic Write free-query figure and the "no premium fee" claim about Gemini inside Google Ads both rest on a single help-page or secondary source rather than being independently cross-confirmed — treat both as approximate and verify directly before relying on them. Meta's Advantage+ AI ad-copy generator is built into Ads Manager at no extra tool cost, but only functions within a live, paid ad campaign — arguably not a "free tool" in the same sense as a standalone product, so it's intentionally left off the list above rather than counted as free.
