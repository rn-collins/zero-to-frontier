ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity — compared honestly

This is decision support, not a ranked "best AI tool" listicle — there isn't one universal winner. Strengths, real limitations, privacy posture, and pricing model for each, so you can match the tool to the task instead of the hype. This hub covers seven general assistants — nine more pages go deep on research, meetings, writing, marketing, automation, coding, images, video, and audio/voice tools specifically.

Head to head

Four tools, four different centers of gravity

OpenAI

ChatGPT

Best use cases

General-purpose assistant, broad plugin/app ecosystem, image generation, voice mode, and the largest surface area of third-party integrations.

Strengths

Most widely adopted, fastest-growing feature set, strong multimodal support (image, voice, file upload), largest user base means the most third-party tutorials and community troubleshooting exist.

Limitations

Free tier has usage caps that reset periodically and shift over time; heaviest feature set can mean more settings to understand before you're using it well.

Privacy considerations

Consumer-tier conversations may be used to improve models unless you opt out in settings, or use Business/Enterprise tiers with different data-handling terms — always check OpenAI's current data-use policy before sharing sensitive information.

Pricing model

Free tier plus paid tiers (historically named Plus/Pro/Business) with higher usage limits and access to newer models first — check OpenAI's own pricing page for current tiers and limits, since these change.

Anthropic

Claude

Best use cases

Long-document analysis and drafting, coding and agentic/computer-use tasks, and tasks where careful instruction-following matters more than raw speed.

Strengths

Strong performance on coding and agentic benchmarks (see the History page's Claude Opus 4.5 entry), large context windows well-suited to pasting in whole documents, generally careful and literal instruction-following which pairs well with the structured prompting on the Techniques page.

Limitations

Smaller third-party plugin ecosystem than ChatGPT; free tier usage limits can be tighter during high-demand periods; no native image-generation model of its own.

Privacy considerations

Anthropic states consumer conversations are not used to train models by default without explicit opt-in — verify current terms, since data-use policies are updated periodically.

Pricing model

Free tier plus paid tiers (historically Pro/Max) with higher message limits and priority access to newest models — check Anthropic's own pricing page for current tiers.

Anthropic's official pricing page ↗
Google

Gemini

Best use cases

Deep integration with Google Workspace (Docs, Sheets, Gmail), native multimodal input including video, and tasks that benefit from Google Search grounding.

Strengths

Tightest integration if you already live in Google Workspace; strong native multimodal handling; Google AI Studio offers a generous free tier for developers experimenting via API.

Limitations

Consumer app experience has historically been less consistent across surfaces (app vs. Workspace side panel vs. AI Studio) than ChatGPT's or Claude's single interface.

Privacy considerations

Data handling differs meaningfully between the free consumer Gemini app, Workspace (business) tiers, and the developer API — check which product you're actually using before assuming a privacy posture.

Pricing model

Free tier, paid consumer tiers bundled with Google One storage plans, and separate Workspace business pricing — check Google's own pricing page, since the bundling structure is more complex than a single flat tier.

Google's official Gemini pricing page ↗
Perplexity

Perplexity

Best use cases

Real-time, cited web research — it's built around search-and-cite rather than being a general chat assistant first.

Strengths

Every answer links its sources by default, making it the strongest fit on this list for the "verify the claim" research pattern described on the Use Cases page; handles current-events questions better than models without live search.

Limitations

Less suited to long, multi-step creative or coding work than ChatGPT or Claude; answer quality depends on the quality of the web sources it finds, which it doesn't fully control.

Privacy considerations

As a search-and-answer product, queries function differently from a private chat assistant — review Perplexity's own privacy policy for how queries and search history are handled.

Pricing model

Free tier with limited "Pro" searches per day, paid Pro tier for higher limits and access to a choice of underlying models — check Perplexity's own pricing page for current limits.

Perplexity's official pricing FAQ ↗

Free-tier limits, pricing, and bundled features change frequently for all four products — treat the descriptions above as directional, not exact, and verify against each provider's own page linked above before deciding.

Also worth knowing

Three more assistants with real, large-scale free usage

These three aren't in the head-to-head above because they're either platform-embedded (Copilot, Meta AI) or newer to mainstream adoption (Grok) — but each has genuinely large free-tier usage worth knowing about.

Microsoft

Microsoft Copilot

Best use cases

Chat with web citations, real-time screen-share help ("Vision"), voice interaction, image generation, and light agentic tasks like booking or shopping — distinct from GitHub Copilot, the separately-branded coding assistant.

Strengths

Free with any Microsoft account; "Think Deeper" reasoning and Voice are free and unlimited; unavoidable in office/education contexts already running Microsoft 365.

Limitations

Word/OneDrive document integrations cap out at 5 files; standalone Copilot Pro subscription was retired for new signups in favor of Microsoft 365 Premium bundling.

Privacy considerations

Trains on de-identified consumer conversation data by default; opt-out available in Profile → Privacy settings (can take up to 30 days). Enterprise M365 Copilot does not train on tenant data.

Pricing model

Free with a Microsoft account; Microsoft 365 Premium at $19.99/mo adds AI credits, extended voice/vision time, and agent tasks; business Microsoft 365 Copilot is $30/user/month billed annually.

Official pricing page ↗
xAI

Grok

Best use cases

Everyday reasoning and chat, real-time X/web-sourced current-events search, a dedicated coding model (Grok Build), and image/video generation (Grok Imagine) — deeply integrated into X.

Strengths

Real-time web/X search and voice are free; large context window (1M tokens on the flagship model); fast-growing adoption as a genuine #3 in US chatbot usage.

Limitations

xAI's own model card documents a real hallucination/dishonesty rate; the product has had multiple public safety incidents with company apologies — worth knowing before relying on it for sensitive topics.

Privacy considerations

Trains on user content by default for consumers; logged-out free users cannot opt out at all. Logged-in users can opt out via Incognito Mode. Enterprise/API customers are excluded from training by default.

Pricing model

Free tier with "generous" but unpublished limits; SuperGrok $30/mo; X Premium tiers ($3-$40/mo) also raise Grok usage limits qualitatively.

Official pricing page ↗
Meta Platforms

Meta AI

Best use cases

Zero-friction everyday assistant embedded directly in WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, and Messenger — quick Q&A, in-chat image generation, writing help, without installing a separate app.

Strengths

Likely the largest reach of any assistant on this page by monthly active users, purely through app-embedding; free across all Meta apps with no published numeric cap.

Limitations

WhatsApp's Meta AI can only read group-chat messages where explicitly @-mentioned — it's never inserted into end-to-end-encrypted 1:1 personal chats. Regional availability is inconsistent even within a single country.

Privacy considerations

As of a December 2025 policy update, Meta AI chat data can be used to personalize ads across Facebook/Instagram/Messenger/WhatsApp, with no separate opt-out — this does not currently apply in the EU, UK, or South Korea.

Pricing model

Free across all Meta apps. A limited "Meta One" subscription test began rolling out in May 2026 in select markets — not yet global, figures still evolving.

Putting it together

How to build a lean AI stack

You don't need all four. Most people are well served by one general assistant and, optionally, one specialist tool.

Pick one general-purpose assistant as your default

Claude or ChatGPT, based on which of the two comparisons above better matches your most common task — long documents and coding lean Claude; broadest ecosystem and multimodal features lean ChatGPT.

Add Perplexity only if verified research is a recurring need

If you regularly need current, cited information rather than the model's training-time knowledge, add Perplexity as a second tool specifically for that — not as a replacement for your default assistant.

Add Gemini only if you're deep in Google Workspace

The integration value is real if your documents already live in Docs/Sheets/Gmail; otherwise a fourth general chat tool usually adds tool-switching cost without a matching benefit.

Re-evaluate roughly twice a year, not weekly

Per the Frontier & AGI page, the model landscape leapfrogs every few weeks — chasing every release adds switching cost with little payoff. Pick a stack, use it well, and revisit deliberately rather than reactively.
