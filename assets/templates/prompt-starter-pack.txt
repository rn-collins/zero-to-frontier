PROMPT STARTER PACK — 10 REUSABLE PROMPTS
Zero → Frontier — free, ungated, no attribution required

Copy any of these and fill in the bracketed parts. See the Techniques
page for why the structure works (XML tags, 5-layer context).

1. MEETING NOTES
Here are my raw notes from today's meeting: [paste notes]
Summarize as: (1) Decisions made, (2) Action items with owner and
deadline if mentioned, (3) Open questions nobody resolved. If an
action item's owner is unclear, list it under "unassigned."

2. EMAIL REPLY
Here's an email I received: [paste email]
My intended answer is: [your answer in plain language]
Draft a reply in [tone] and [length].

3. RESEARCH SYNTHESIS
Here are [N] sources on [topic]: [paste or list them]
Synthesize into: (1) where they agree, (2) where they meaningfully
disagree, (3) any claim only one source makes. Attribute every claim
to its source by name.

4. LEARNING A NEW TOPIC
Teach me [topic] assuming I know [your real background].
Explain the core idea in plain language, then ask me one question to
check I understood before going further. Tell me explicitly where
this field has real, unsettled disagreement among experts.

5. DOCUMENT COMPARISON
Compare Document A and Document B: [paste both]
List only substantive changes — dates, amounts, added or removed
obligations. For each, quote the exact before and after text. Ignore
purely stylistic wording changes.

6. CODE DEBUGGING
I'm getting this error: [paste exact error]
Here's the relevant code: [paste code]
Explain what's causing it, then give me the fix. I want to
understand the cause, not just copy a patch.

7. BRAINSTORMING
I need [N] options for [thing]. Audience: [who]. Tone: [tone].
Already rejected: [list]. Give me a wide, varied first pass — don't
converge on one style yet.

8. WRITING EDIT PASS
<role>You are a [type] editor for [audience].</role>
<context>[relevant background on style/past feedback]</context>
<task>Edit the attached draft for clarity and tone.</task>
<instructions>
1. Flag jargon and define or replace it.
2. Keep sentences under 25 words where possible.
3. Preserve the author's voice — don't over-formalize.
</instructions>
<constraints>Do not change any numbers or factual claims.</constraints>
<output_format>Return the edited draft only, no commentary.</output_format>

9. META-PROMPT (WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW HOW TO ASK YET)
I want a prompt that will make you [goal].
Here are examples of what I want: [paste 2-3 examples]
Write me the ideal prompt to get this consistently, including what
tone, structure, and format to specify.

10. DECISION SUPPORT (NOT A DECISION)
Here's a decision I'm weighing: [describe it]
Constraints: [list them]
Lay out the strongest case for each option and the biggest risk of
each — don't tell me which to pick, help me see the tradeoffs clearly.
