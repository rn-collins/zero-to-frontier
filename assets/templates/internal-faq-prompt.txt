Zero → Frontier — free, ungated, no attribution required

HOW TO USE THIS: Paste in the recurring questions your team actually
asks (from Slack, email, or memory) and the AI structures them into
a proper internal FAQ document.

---

I want to build an internal FAQ for [team/topic, e.g. "our expense
reimbursement process" or "how we handle client requests"].

HERE ARE THE QUESTIONS PEOPLE ACTUALLY ASK, roughly, with whatever
answer I know off the top of my head (some may be incomplete —
flag those):
[list as many real recurring questions as you can, with rough answers]

Turn this into a structured FAQ:
1. Group related questions together under clear headings.
2. Write each answer clearly and completely — if my rough answer was
   incomplete, flag exactly what's missing rather than inventing the
   missing part.
3. Order questions by how often they likely come up, most common first.
4. Add a "last updated" placeholder and a note on who to ask if the
   FAQ doesn't cover a new question.

Flag any question where you don't have enough information from me to
give a confident answer, rather than guessing.
